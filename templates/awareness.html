Brain Tumor Awareness and Early Action

Educational guidance on warning signs, risk awareness, and why timely neurological consultation matters.

Early Warning Signs

  • Persistent headaches
  • New seizures
  • Vision or speech change
  • Unusual weakness or numbness

When to Seek Care

  • Symptoms worsening over days
  • Sudden neurological deficits
  • Confusion or behavior change
  • Recurrent unexplained vomiting

Risk Awareness

  • Family or genetic predisposition
  • Past cranial radiation exposure
  • Long-standing neurological complaints
  • Immunocompromised status

Healthy Habits

  • Regular checkups
  • Sleep and stress management
  • Physical activity and nutrition
  • Avoid delayed symptom reporting

Educational Note

This page provides general health awareness content only. It does not replace medical evaluation, imaging, or specialist consultation.
