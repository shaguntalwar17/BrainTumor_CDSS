# WHO CNS Tumor Reference Notes (Academic Context)

These notes are an academic summary used only for retrieval-augmented context in this prototype.
They are not a replacement for official WHO publications or clinical judgement.

Neuro-oncology assessment generally combines imaging, pathology, molecular markers, and longitudinal follow-up.
Imaging alone should not be used to assign definitive diagnosis or treatment plans.

MRI interpretation often benefits from multi-modal sequences (for example T1, T1 post-contrast, T2, and FLAIR),
because edema, enhancing tissue, and necrotic components can present differently by modality.

Longitudinal review should compare scan dates, acquisition protocols, and segmentation consistency before inferring progression.
Apparent changes can also arise from scanner differences, preprocessing differences, and registration artifacts.

Confidence and explainability maps are supportive signals, not clinical proof.
Any low-confidence prediction or poor explainability-to-mask overlap requires expert review.

Final clinical decisions require qualified radiology and oncology review, correlation with symptoms, and full medical history.
